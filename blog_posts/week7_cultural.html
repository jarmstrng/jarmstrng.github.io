The Value of Continued Learning

Assignment: Identify values that are important to you. Explain how you will use affirmation to help you succeed.

When you think of the times in your life where you've been the happiest, the proudest, or the most satisfied, which values come to mind?

Personally: learning, growth, integrity. Interpersonally: curiosity, helping.

Pick one value and write a couple of sentences about why one of the values you chose is important to you.

I believe that learning, growth, and curiosity are three interrelated values that have shaped my life in the ways that I am most proud of. A voracious appetite for learning has resulted in:

  • reading or skimming every book relating to sustainable architecture (even tangentially) in my small-town library (plus plenty of books on other subjects)
  • embracing MOOCs as soon as they became available
  • using internet resources to teach myself how to fill a role as a professional digital content manager
  • traveling to 13 countries in 12 months on a modest budget
  • successfully teaching preschoolers developmental gymnastics and karate despite having no background
  • going from no experience with the Spanish language to functional in countries with few English speakers

Without this curiosity and impulse to learn more, my life up to this point would not have been nearly as interesting or fulfilling.

What was the last topic that someone asked for your advice on?

I recently had to teach my former coworkers how to take over my job duties, and so I spent several hours showing them how to use WordPress, photo editing software, and Constant Contact's email marketing services - all things I had to teach myself when I first started the job, as the position didn't exist at that company until I was hired. It was necessary not only to teach the skills, but to encourage them to adopt an exploratory mindset and not be afraid to poke around with the tools to see what could be done.

If the stereotype is one that suggests I might be incapable of reaching my goals, remembering my history of success with learning, integrity, and helping would likely lessen that threat.

As "helping" is important to me, I have found that existential angst and many frustrations can be decreased via charitable donations to causes I feel strongly about.
